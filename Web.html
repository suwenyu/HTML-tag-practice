課程目標 Course objectives

The course aims to provide graduate students of the TIGP program of Social Network and Human-Centered Computing (SNHCC) an overview of human-centered computing (HCC), particularly from the point of view of user-oriented computing system design and research. As an emerging, multidisciplinary field, HCC is commonly referred to and characterized by the idea of devising designs of computing systems based on the properties, needs and constraints of the users and their tasks, rather than the inverse. Thus HCC embodies not only technology building, such as how to the software and hardware systems for supporting people, but also systematic understanding of people and the interactions between people and technologies.

課程大綱 Course Description

In this course, we’ll explain and illustrate the state of the art of human-centered computing, focusing on the fundamental concepts and practices of human-computer interaction design and engineering, methods for studying users/tasks, methods for design-prototyping, and key topics that are closely related to the TIGP program, such as social computing, mobile computing, and tangible interface etc.
